<Info>
  **Research articles vs. courses:** A research article is a single, long-form explainer on one focused topic — think of it as a detailed essay or technical deep-dive. A course, by contrast, is a multi-module curriculum broken into structured lessons with progress tracking and quizzes. Use research articles for quick mastery of a specific concept; use courses for end-to-end learning journeys.
</Info>

## Generating research articles

With a free Coursify account you can generate your own research articles on any topic using the Research mode — free accounts include 5 AI generations per month shared across all modes.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the dashboard">
    Navigate to [coursify.hasanraiyan.me/dashboard](https://coursify.hasanraiyan.me/dashboard) and sign in.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch to Research mode">
    Use the mode switcher to select **Research** before entering your topic. This tells Coursify's agents to produce a single deep-dive article rather than a condensed Flash guide.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Submit your topic">
    Type your topic and click generate. The agents scan verified web sources, compile references, and write the article in seconds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

For more detail on the Research mode workflow, including tips on phrasing topics for the best output, see the [Research Mode guide](/features/research-mode).

## Reading and sharing

All published research articles are publicly accessible at [coursify.hasanraiyan.me/research](https://coursify.hasanraiyan.me/research) — copy and share any article URL freely. Articles render in Coursify's rich reader, which supports:

- **Markdown formatting** — headings, lists, tables, blockquotes, and bold/italic text.
- **Syntax-highlighted code blocks** — for any programming language.
- **LaTeX math rendering** — inline and block-level equations displayed beautifully.

<Note>
  Pro plan subscribers can also listen to any research article with **Audio speak mode** and export articles to **Markdown or PDF** for offline study. See the [plans page](/billing/plans) for details.
</Note>
