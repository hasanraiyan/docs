Coursify doesn't present AI-generated content as a plain wall of text. The rich reader renders every course section and research article with the typographic and interactive components that technical and academic material actually demands — formatted headings and tables, properly typeset math, colour-coded code, and instant definitions on demand. Whether you're working through algorithm proofs or studying distributed systems architecture, the reading experience is built to match the complexity of the subject.

## What the rich reader supports

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Rich Markdown" icon="type">
    Full Markdown rendering with hierarchical headings, tables, callout blocks, bold/italic emphasis, numbered and bulleted lists. Complex ideas are structured for skimmability, not buried in paragraphs.
  </Card>
  <Card title="LaTeX math formulas" icon="sigma">
    Inline math (e.g., `O(n log n)`) and full block equations are rendered using LaTeX. Essential for algorithm complexity analysis, recurrence relations, probability, and any course with formal mathematical content.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Syntax-highlighted code" icon="code">
    Code samples are syntax-highlighted across multiple languages — Python, Java, JavaScript, C++, SQL, and more. Algorithm implementations and system design snippets are presented with the clarity of an IDE.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hoverable keyword definitions" icon="mouse-pointer">
    Hover over any highlighted keyword to see a concise definition in a tooltip, without leaving the page. Useful for technical terminology, acronyms, and domain-specific vocabulary encountered mid-reading.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Navigating a course

The rich reader includes a persistent **sidebar module list** on the left. Every module and its sections are listed in order, and you can click any section to jump to it directly — no need to navigate back to the course overview page.

As you work through the course, Coursify tracks your **progress** per section. Completed sections are visually marked in the sidebar, giving you an at-a-glance view of how far through the course you are and which sections you still need to cover. Progress is saved automatically and synced to your [dashboard](/library/dashboard).

## Audio read-aloud (Pro)

<Info>
  Audio read-aloud is a **Pro plan** feature. See the [plans page](/billing/plans) for availability and pricing.
</Info>

Pro users get a **speak mode** that reads course content aloud using AI-generated audio. Activate it from the section toolbar to have the text read to you while you follow along — or close the tab and just listen. Speak mode is particularly useful for auditory learners, for reviewing material during a commute, or for giving your eyes a break during a long study session.

## Export to Markdown & PDF (Pro)

<Info>
  Markdown and PDF export is a **Pro plan** feature. See the [plans page](/billing/plans) for availability and pricing.
</Info>

Pro users can export any course section, full course, or research article to either:

- **Markdown** — a portable `.md` file that you can open in any editor, paste into Notion, Obsidian, or a GitHub README, or version-control alongside your own notes
- **PDF** — a formatted document ready for printing, offline reading, or sharing with teammates and classmates who don't use Coursify

Export is available from the course overview page and from the rich reader toolbar.

## Shared workspace notebooks (Scholar)

Scholar plan users can collaborate in **shared workspace notebooks** — a shared environment where study groups, classrooms, or research teams can access and annotate courses together. If you're on the Scholar plan, your shared notebooks are accessible directly from the [dashboard](/library/dashboard).

<Note>
  LaTeX rendering is especially valuable in math-heavy courses. The **Design and Analysis of Algorithms (DAA)** course, for example, uses block equations extensively for recurrence relations, complexity proofs, and NP-completeness reductions. Similarly, **Compiler Design** relies on formal grammar notation throughout. Without proper LaTeX support, this content would be unreadable — the rich reader renders it exactly as you'd see it in a university textbook.
</Note>
