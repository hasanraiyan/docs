Research mode generates a single, comprehensive, source-backed article on any topic you specify — without building a full multi-module course. It uses the same real-time AI research agents that power course generation, but the output is a standalone deep-dive document rather than a structured curriculum. Use it when you need a thorough explainer fast, without the overhead of navigating a full course.

## Research vs Course mode

Both modes use Coursify's AI research pipeline, but they produce very different outputs.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Research mode" icon="file-text">
    Generates a **single, long-form article** — great for concept explainers, technology comparisons, learning roadmaps, and interview-prep briefs. Fastest way to go deep on one topic.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Course mode" icon="graduation-cap">
    Generates a **full multi-module curriculum** — structured modules, per-section content, learning objectives, prerequisites, and quizzes. Best for in-depth, progressive learning over days or weeks.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

As a rule of thumb: reach for **Research** when you want a single reference document you can read in one sitting. Switch to **Course** when you want a structured path that takes you from beginner to proficient.

## What Research articles look like

Research articles are rendered with the same rich components as course sections — full Markdown formatting, inline and block LaTeX for mathematical content, and syntax-highlighted code blocks. Published articles are publicly readable at [coursify.hasanraiyan.me/research](https://coursify.hasanraiyan.me/research) with no account required.

Examples of live research articles on the platform include:

- **Docker vs Kubernetes** — a comprehensive comparison of containerisation vs orchestration, covering use-cases, architecture, and a decision framework
- **Getting Started with ArgoCD** — a GitOps continuous delivery deep-dive covering installation, reconciliation loops, and best practices
- **tRPC Crash Course** — end-to-end type-safe API design in TypeScript, covering routers, procedures, context, and Next.js integration
- **Learn JavaScript in 30 Days** — a structured 30-day roadmap from syntax fundamentals to async/await and DOM projects
- **AWS vs Azure** — a side-by-side cloud platform comparison covering market share, compute, storage, hybrid capabilities, and TCO

Topics range from technical deep-dives and language crash courses to salary guides, scientific introductions (e.g., *Introduction to Superconductivity*), and interview-preparation briefs.

## Generating a research article

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the dashboard or homepage">
    Navigate to [coursify.hasanraiyan.me](https://coursify.hasanraiyan.me) or open your [dashboard](/library/dashboard). You'll see the topic input and mode selector.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Switch the mode toggle to Research">
    Look for the **Course / Research** toggle above the input field. Select **Research**. This tells Coursify to produce a single article rather than a full course.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your topic">
    Type a specific topic, question, comparison, or roadmap prompt. The more precise your input, the more targeted the article — for example, *"Docker vs Kubernetes for a small team"* will produce a sharper result than just *"containers"*.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Coursify generates and saves the article">
    The AI agents research the web, write the article, and save it to your library. The article is also published to the public research feed, where anyone can read it without an account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Public research library

Every Research article you generate is added to the public library at [coursify.hasanraiyan.me/research](https://coursify.hasanraiyan.me/research). Articles are free to read by anyone — no sign-in, no paywall. This makes Research mode a convenient way to share knowledge with teammates, classmates, or anyone you'd normally send a link to.

Your personal library in the [dashboard](/library/research-articles) also keeps a private list of every article you've generated, so you can revisit them without searching the public feed.

<Tip>
  Research mode shines for **technology comparisons** (AWS vs Azure, REST vs GraphQL), **learning roadmaps** (Learn Python in 30 Days, Learn AI in 90 Days), and **interview-prep topics** (Systems Programmer Interview Preparation, Pass Coding Interviews). Run a quick Research article to orient yourself before deciding whether to generate a full course on the same subject.
</Tip>
